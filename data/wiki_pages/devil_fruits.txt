Devil Fruits

Devil Fruits are mysterious fruits found throughout the world of One Piece. When eaten, they grant the consumer a unique supernatural power or ability at the cost of their ability to swim. The sea rejects Devil Fruit users, rendering them permanently unable to float or swim in seawater, and exposure to seawater weakens them significantly. Devil Fruits are considered the treasure of the sea and can fetch extremely high prices on the black market.

Only one Devil Fruit can be eaten per person. Eating a second Devil Fruit will result in the consumer's death, as the two Devil Fruit spirits within a single body will destroy each other. The only known exception to this rule is Marshall D. Teach (Blackbeard), who through an unknown mechanism became the only person in recorded history to possess two Devil Fruit powers simultaneously.

Types of Devil Fruits

Devil Fruits are categorized into three types: Paramecia, Zoan, and Logia.

Paramecia

Paramecia-type Devil Fruits are the most common type. They grant the user a variety of superhuman abilities that do not fall into the other two categories. These abilities range from body modification (such as rubber-body or blade-body) to environmental manipulation (such as creating earthquakes or controlling strings) to producing or transforming substances (such as wax, poison, or barriers).

Notable Paramecia users include:
- Monkey D. Luffy (Gomu Gomu no Mi / Hito Hito no Mi, Model: Nika): grants rubber-body properties with awakening allowing environmental rubber transformation; originally believed to be the Gomu Gomu no Mi, later revealed to be one of the most sought-after fruits in history
- Edward Newgate / Whitebeard (Gura Gura no Mi): creates powerful shockwaves and vibrations capable of generating tsunamis and earthquakes; considered the most destructive Paramecia
- Trafalgar Law (Ope Ope no Mi): creates a spherical "Room" within which the user can rearrange anything spatially; also capable of performing surgical operations impossible by conventional medicine
- Donquixote Doflamingo (Ito Ito no Mi): generates and controls strings strong enough to cut through stone and steel; capable of controlling people like puppets by threading strings into their bodies

Zoan

Zoan-type Devil Fruits allow the user to transform into an animal or a human-animal hybrid. Standard Zoan fruits grant three forms: the user's base form, a full animal form, and a hybrid form that combines human and animal characteristics. Zoan users generally experience increased physical strength and endurance.

Mythical Zoan fruits, a rare subtype, grant transformation into legendary creatures and are rarer than even Logia fruits. They typically grant additional special abilities beyond simple transformation, such as regeneration, fire-breathing, or flight.

Ancient Zoan fruits grant transformation into extinct prehistoric animals, typically granting superior raw power compared to standard Zoan.

Notable Zoan users include:
- Marco the Phoenix (Tori Tori no Mi, Model: Phoenix): a Mythical Zoan granting phoenix transformation and regenerative blue flames
- Sengoku (Hito Hito no Mi, Model: Daibutsu): a Mythical Zoan granting transformation into a great golden Buddha with enormous striking power
- X Drake (Ryu Ryu no Mi, Model: Allosaurus): an Ancient Zoan granting transformation into an allosaurus

Logia

Logia-type Devil Fruits are the rarest and generally considered the most powerful. They allow the user to transform their body into, generate, and control a natural element or force. A Logia user's body actually becomes the element — physical attacks pass through them harmlessly unless the attacker uses Haki or a substance that naturally counters the element.

Notable Logia users include:
- Portgas D. Ace (Mera Mera no Mi): fire; the flame element is countered by Akainu's magma
- Admiral Akainu / Sakazuki (Magu Magu no Mi): magma; considered the most offensive Logia power due to magma's temperature exceeding fire
- Admiral Aokiji / Kuzan (Hie Hie no Mi): ice; can freeze vast areas of ocean solid
- Admiral Kizaru / Borsalino (Pika Pika no Mi): light; allows movement and attacks at the speed of light
- Smoker (Moku Moku no Mi): smoke; can expand and control smoke to capture opponents

Haki as a Counter to Logia

Haki is a power that exists in all living things and can be trained to become a combat tool. Busoshoku Haki (Armament Haki) allows the user to coat their body or weapons in an invisible armor that can make physical contact with Logia users as though they were solid. This is the primary counter to Logia intangibility. Advanced Busoshoku Haki allows the user to project this armor outward to damage the interior of targets without touching the surface.

Devil Fruit Awakening

Some Devil Fruit users are able to awaken their powers to a higher level. Awakening extends the user's power beyond their own body. For Paramecia users, awakening typically allows the user to transform the surrounding environment — rocks, ground, and structures — into their element or substance. For Zoan users, awakening grants enhanced physical abilities and faster recovery. The Gomu Gomu no Mi's true awakening, revealed to be the Hito Hito no Mi, Model: Nika, grants Luffy the ability to transform his surroundings into rubber and use them with complete freedom of movement, described as the most ridiculous power in the world.

Seastone

Seastone (Kairoseki) is a naturally occurring mineral found in the One Piece world that replicates the effect of seawater on Devil Fruit users. Objects made from or coated in Seastone weaken Devil Fruit users on contact, negating their powers. The Marines use Seastone handcuffs to restrain captured Devil Fruit users, including Ace during his imprisonment at Impel Down and his transport to Marineford. Ace's powers were suppressed during his imprisonment until Luffy removed the handcuffs during the battle.

Reincarnation of Devil Fruits

When a Devil Fruit user dies, their power does not disappear permanently. The power reincarnates into the nearest fruit, which then transforms to resemble the original Devil Fruit. This is why Devil Fruits that belonged to deceased users can be found and eaten again. After Ace's death, his Mera Mera no Mi reincarnated and was eventually obtained by Sabo, who ate it to inherit his brother's power.
