Edward Newgate (Whitebeard)

Edward Newgate, commonly known as Whitebeard, was the captain of the Whitebeard Pirates and one of the Four Emperors who ruled over the second half of the Grand Line, known as the New World. He was recognized by the World Government as the World's Strongest Man and the man closest to claiming the One Piece. He died at the Battle of Marineford.

Appearance and Reputation

Whitebeard was an enormous man, one of the largest characters in the series, with a massive build, a white crescent-shaped mustache that gave him his name, and a scar across his chest from a lifetime of battles. In his prime he was considered without equal in combat. By the time of Marineford, he was elderly and in poor health, relying on medical life support and IVs between battles, yet even in this condition he was capable of fighting the entire Marine force including three Admirals.

His reputation was so significant that the World Government deliberately scheduled Ace's public execution to use as bait — the only way to draw Whitebeard out of the New World and into a direct confrontation on Marine terms.

Devil Fruit: Gura Gura no Mi

Whitebeard ate the Gura Gura no Mi (Tremor-Tremor Fruit), a Paramecia-type Devil Fruit that granted him the ability to generate powerful vibrations, or tremors, that propagated through any medium — air, water, ground, or structures. The Gorosei, the highest authority in the World Government, described the Gura Gura no Mi as capable of destroying the world itself.

In practice, Whitebeard used this power to:
- Generate shockwaves powerful enough to split the air
- Tilt the entire island of Marineford by striking the ground
- Create tsunamis by generating vibrations in the ocean
- Collapse portions of Marineford's defensive walls during the battle

Even in his weakened state, a single strike from Whitebeard was capable of devastating a large area. During the battle he fought Akainu directly and landed blows against the Admiral despite both combatants being Logia-class powers — possible because Whitebeard's Haki coated his strikes.

Relationship with His Crew

Whitebeard referred to his crew members as his sons and daughters. This was not a formality — he treated them with genuine paternal affection and considered their well-being his personal responsibility. He had over 1,600 pirates under his direct command and 43 subordinate crews, yet he was known personally to his commanders and treated the relationships as family bonds rather than hierarchical obligations.

His relationship with Ace was particularly significant. Ace initially joined the crew with the goal of eventually fighting and defeating Whitebeard to claim the title of the world's greatest pirate. After working alongside Whitebeard and understanding his character, Ace abandoned this goal and came to genuinely respect and love him as a father figure. When asked near his death whether he had any regrets about following Whitebeard, Ace's answer was unambiguous — none.

When Ace died at Marineford, Whitebeard, despite his own critical injuries, continued fighting and took direct vengeance on Akainu, landing a powerful strike on the Admiral before his final stand.

Death and Legacy

Whitebeard died at Marineford after sustaining injuries that would have killed any other person long before the battle ended. Medical accounts from the series describe him as having been struck by 267 sword wounds, 152 gunshot wounds, and 46 cannon blasts, in addition to direct hits from Admiral-level attacks including magma. He died standing upright — his body never collapsed forward, which the series treats as deeply symbolic of his character.

His final words were a declaration that the One Piece is real, spoken loudly enough for the assembled world to hear. This statement, broadcast globally, confirmed to a new generation of pirates that the treasure exists and the age of piracy was not over.

After his death, the territories he had protected from invasion became unguarded, triggering immediate conflict. Formerly subordinate crews and outside parties began competing for control of these territories in an event called the Scramble for Whitebeard's Territories. This period of upheaval is considered one of the major factors shaping the current balance of power in the New World.

His Devil Fruit power was later absorbed by Blackbeard through an unknown mechanism — the same man who had betrayed Ace and handed him to the Marines. Blackbeard's acquisition of the Gura Gura no Mi in addition to his existing Yami Yami no Mi made him the only confirmed person in history to hold two Devil Fruit abilities simultaneously.
