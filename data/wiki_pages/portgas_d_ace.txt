Portgas D. Ace

Portgas D. Ace, born as Gol D. Ace, was a major character in the One Piece series. He was the second division commander of the Whitebeard Pirates and the adoptive older brother of Monkey D. Luffy and Sabo. He was the biological son of the late Pirate King, Gol D. Roger, and Portgas D. Rouge. Ace was one of the most powerful pirates of his generation before his death at Marineford.

Appearance and Personality

Ace was a tall, muscular young man with short black hair and dark eyes. He was almost always depicted with his trademark orange hat, a beaded necklace, and an open shirt or no shirt at all. He had tattoos on his back that read "ASCE," with the S crossed out — a tribute to his friend Sabo, who was believed dead at the time. His left arm bore a tattoo of the Whitebeard Pirates' Jolly Roger.

Ace was known for his carefree and headstrong personality. He was deeply loyal to Whitebeard, whom he came to regard as a true father figure after years of questioning whether he deserved to exist at all. His greatest personal struggle was the belief, stemming from his birth as Roger's son, that he was cursed — that a man as hated as Roger should never have had a child. This belief shaped his character profoundly, making him fiercely protective of anyone who treated him and his brothers with kindness.

Despite his confident exterior, Ace carried significant guilt and existential doubt throughout his life. His last words at Marineford — expressing surprise and gratitude that people had loved him — reveal how deep this insecurity ran.

Devil Fruit: Mera Mera no Mi

Ace ate the Mera Mera no Mi (Flame-Flame Fruit), a Logia-type Devil Fruit that allowed him to create, control, and transform his body into fire. As a Logia user, physical attacks passed through his fire body without dealing damage, unless the attacker used Haki or a substance that could counter his element. His fire abilities were considered among the most powerful Logia in the series.

Ace's signature techniques included:
- Fire Fist (Hiken): A massive fist of fire launched forward, capable of destroying ships
- Flame Commandment (Enjoji): A sweeping fire attack covering a wide area
- Great Flame Commandment — Hibashira: A massive pillar of fire erupting from the ground
- Flame Emperor (Enkai — Entei): One of his most powerful attacks, creating an enormous sphere of fire

The Mera Mera no Mi was ultimately countered by Admiral Akainu's Magu Magu no Mi, as magma burns at a higher temperature than fire and can overwhelm Ace's flames directly. This elemental disadvantage proved fatal at Marineford.

After Ace's death, the Mera Mera no Mi was eventually found and eaten by Sabo, Ace and Luffy's other adoptive brother, who survived the incident that Luffy and Ace had believed killed him.

Early Life and Connection to Roger

Ace's mother, Portgas D. Rouge, carried him in her womb for twenty months through sheer willpower to protect him from the World Government, who sought to eliminate all of Gol D. Roger's offspring. Rouge died shortly after Ace's birth. Ace was entrusted to Monkey D. Garp, who raised him alongside Luffy in Foosha Village under the name Portgas D. Ace — his mother's surname — rather than Gol D. Ace, to hide his heritage.

As a child, Ace met Sabo and Luffy, and the three became brothers by sharing sake, a traditional ceremony in the One Piece world. Their brotherhood was forged through shared adventures and a shared desire to become free pirates. Sabo was believed killed by a World Noble's ship when the three were still young, and his apparent death left a lasting mark on both Ace and Luffy.

Ace set out to sea before Luffy and quickly rose to prominence, eventually joining the Whitebeard Pirates and becoming one of Whitebeard's most valued commanders.

Capture and Execution

Ace pursued Marshall D. Teach (Blackbeard) after Teach killed a fellow Whitebeard crewmate and fled — an unforgivable act within the crew. The two fought on Banaro Island, where Teach's Yami Yami no Mi (Dark-Dark Fruit) proved capable of nullifying Ace's Devil Fruit powers, as the darkness absorbed all attacks. Teach defeated Ace and turned him over to the Marines in exchange for a Warlord of the Sea title.

Ace was imprisoned in Level 6 of Impel Down, the world's most secure prison, and sentenced to public execution at Marineford. His execution was timed to occur when Whitebeard's rescue attempt was anticipated to be at its peak, as a deliberate strategy by the World Government.

His death at Marineford at the hands of Akainu came not because he failed to escape, but because he could not leave without defending Whitebeard's name. When Akainu insulted Whitebeard in Ace's presence, Ace turned back — despite being free, despite Luffy's plea — and the decision cost him his life.

Legacy

Ace's death had profound effects on several characters. Luffy lost consciousness following Ace's death and went through a period of profound grief and self-doubt before resolving to return to the sea stronger. Sabo, who had survived and joined the Revolutionary Army without his memories, eventually recovered his memories of Ace and Luffy and inherited the Mera Mera no Mi in Ace's honor. Whitebeard's death at the same battle, witnessed by the world, marked the end of an era.

Ace is remembered throughout the One Piece world as a powerful and principled pirate. His vivre card — a piece of paper connected to a person's life force — was kept by Luffy throughout the series as a memento.
