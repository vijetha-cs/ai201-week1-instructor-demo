Haki

Haki is a mysterious power that exists in every living being in the One Piece world. Most people never develop it, and even among those who do, only a fraction learn to use it in combat. Haki is described as a manifestation of the user's spirit — a power that goes beyond physical strength and Devil Fruit abilities. It is one of the few forces capable of countering Logia-type Devil Fruit intangibility.

The concept of Haki was known to exist long before it was formally explained in the series, referenced through phenomena such as the "Color of the Supreme King" or the "Spirit" that certain powerful characters projected. It was formally introduced and named during the Marineford Arc and the subsequent training timeskip.

Types of Haki

There are three main types of Haki: Kenbunshoku Haki, Busoshoku Haki, and Haoshoku Haki.

Kenbunshoku Haki (Observation Haki)

Kenbunshoku Haki, also known as Observation Haki or the Color of Observation, grants the user a form of extrasensory perception. At its most basic level it allows the user to sense the presence, strength, and emotions of others — functioning as a kind of awareness that extends beyond normal sight and hearing. Experienced users can anticipate attacks before they occur by reading the intent of the attacker. At its most advanced level, called Future Sight, the user can briefly see into the near future, allowing them to dodge attacks that have not yet been thrown.

Kenbunshoku Haki cannot be used while unconscious or in a severely compromised mental state. It also has a known weakness: a user who deliberately acts without thinking — such as a child or someone who fights on instinct alone — may be harder to predict, as there is less intent to read.

Busoshoku Haki (Armament Haki)

Busoshoku Haki, also known as Armament Haki or the Color of Arms, allows the user to generate an invisible armor from their spirit that coats their body or weapons. This armor increases the strength of physical attacks and, critically, allows the user to make physical contact with Logia-type Devil Fruit users as though they were solid, bypassing their elemental intangibility.

When Busoshoku Haki is applied, the coated area typically darkens in color and takes on a hardened appearance. The degree of hardening corresponds to the skill and power of the user. Advanced Busoshoku Haki allows the user to project this armor outward in a technique called Ryou (flowing Haki), which penetrates the surface of targets to damage them from the inside without requiring direct surface contact. This advanced technique is particularly effective against armored or otherwise hardened opponents.

Busoshoku Haki can be applied to weapons, making them significantly more powerful. Marine weapons and the weapons of skilled pirates are often described as being "coated" in Haki during serious combat.

Haoshoku Haki (Conqueror's Haki)

Haoshoku Haki, also known as Conqueror's Haki or the Color of the Supreme King, is the rarest form of Haki. It cannot be learned or developed through training — a person either has the potential for it from birth or they do not. It is estimated that only one in several million people possess this ability.

Haoshoku Haki allows the user to project their willpower outward, overwhelming the consciousness of weaker-willed individuals in the vicinity. A powerful burst of Haoshoku Haki can render large numbers of people unconscious simultaneously. Only individuals with particularly strong willpower or their own Haki can resist being knocked out.

Monkey D. Luffy demonstrated Haoshoku Haki at Marineford when, overwhelmed with emotion and desperation to save Ace, he unconsciously released a massive burst that knocked out a large portion of the Marine forces and several pirates simultaneously. This was widely recognized as a display of Haoshoku Haki at a scale rarely seen. Both enemies and allies were stunned by the demonstration, as it confirmed Luffy's potential to become a dominant force in the pirate world.

Advanced Haoshoku Haki, displayed by the most powerful users, allows the user to coat their body or attacks with the Conqueror's energy — functioning similarly to Busoshoku Haki but carrying significantly more force. This level is associated with the strongest characters in the series.

Haki and Devil Fruits

Haki and Devil Fruit powers are not mutually exclusive — a Devil Fruit user can also develop and use Haki, and many of the most powerful characters in the series combine both. Busoshoku Haki is particularly important in combat against Logia users, as it negates the otherwise near-invulnerable intangibility of Logia transformations.

During the Marineford Arc, Luffy's use of Haki — particularly Haoshoku Haki — marked a turning point in how the world perceived him. Prior to this, he was known as a capable but unconventional pirate. The scale of his Haoshoku Haki release at Marineford changed that perception among those who witnessed it, signaling that he had the qualities of a future Emperor.
