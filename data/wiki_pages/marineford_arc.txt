Marineford Arc

The Marineford Arc is the twenty-second story arc in the One Piece series and the fourth arc in the Summit War Saga. It covers the Battle of Marineford, one of the most significant conflicts in the history of the One Piece world, fought between the Marines and the Whitebeard Pirates along with their allies.

Overview

The arc begins immediately after the Impel Down Arc, with Monkey D. Luffy and a group of escaped prisoners arriving at Marineford aboard a stolen Marine ship. Their goal is to rescue Portgas D. Ace, Luffy's adoptive brother, who has been sentenced to public execution by the World Government. Ace is the second division commander of the Whitebeard Pirates and the son of the late Pirate King Gol D. Roger, a fact the World Government has used to justify his execution as a symbol of their power.

The execution is scheduled to take place at Marineford, the headquarters of the Marines and the seat of the Three Admirals. Fleet Admiral Sengoku and the three Admirals — Akainu (Sakazuki), Aokiji (Kuzan), and Kizaru (Borsalino) — are all present, along with the Seven Warlords of the Sea, several of whom have allied with the Marines for this engagement.

The Whitebeard Pirates and their massive allied fleet launch a full-scale assault on Marineford to rescue Ace before the execution can be carried out. Edward Newgate, known as Whitebeard and recognized as the World's Strongest Man, leads the assault personally despite being in failing health.

The Battle

The battle begins with the Whitebeard Pirates breaching the defensive walls of Marineford. Whitebeard's Devil Fruit ability, the Gura Gura no Mi (Tremor-Tremor Fruit), allows him to create powerful shockwaves capable of generating tsunamis and earthquakes. He uses this power to collapse the walls and flood portions of the plaza.

The Seven Warlords engage the Whitebeard Pirates' commanders in individual battles throughout the plaza. Mihawk Dracule, the world's greatest swordsman, cuts through waves of pirates. Bartholomew Kuma fires blasts at the advancing fleet. Donquixote Doflamingo operates with characteristic unpredictability, at one point attempting to take control of Jozu, the third division commander of the Whitebeard Pirates.

Luffy and the escaped prisoners from Impel Down fight their way through the Marine forces toward the execution platform. Luffy, despite having no prior military training or tactical experience, charges forward using his Gear Second and Gear Third abilities. His reckless advance repeatedly draws the attention of the Admirals.

Admiral Kizaru attempts to stop Luffy using his Pika Pika no Mi (Glint-Glint Fruit), which allows him to move and attack at the speed of light. Silvers Rayleigh, the former first mate of the Roger Pirates, appears unexpectedly at Marineford and intercepts Kizaru, buying Luffy critical time.

The Execution of Ace

As Luffy reaches the execution platform, Fleet Admiral Sengoku reveals to the assembled crowd that Ace is the son of Gol D. Roger. Sengoku transforms using his Hito Hito no Mi, Model: Daibutsu (Human-Human Fruit, Model: Great Buddha) and attacks. Garp, Luffy's grandfather and a Marine Vice Admiral, stands at the platform but ultimately steps aside when Luffy confronts him, unable to stop his own grandson.

Luffy uses his most powerful technique at the time, Gear Second combined with Haoshoku Haki — a rare form of Haki that only one in several million people possess — to knock out a large portion of the Marine forces in a single burst. This stuns both allies and enemies, as Haoshoku Haki at this scale had rarely been displayed publicly.

Ace is temporarily freed from his Seastone handcuffs by Luffy. However, the moment Ace's powers are restored, Akainu provokes him by insulting Whitebeard's name and legacy. Unable to let the insult stand, Ace turns back to confront Akainu instead of fleeing with Luffy.

Death of Ace and Whitebeard

Admiral Akainu's Devil Fruit, the Magu Magu no Mi (Magma-Magma Fruit), grants him the ability to create and control magma. When Akainu attacks Luffy, Ace steps in front of the blow, taking a magma fist through the chest to protect his brother. Ace's Mera Mera no Mi (Flame-Flame Fruit) powers are countered entirely by Akainu's magma, as magma burns hotter than fire.

Ace dies in Luffy's arms, his final words expressing gratitude for the love he received from Luffy, Whitebeard, and their crew. Ace had long struggled with doubts about whether his life had value, given that he was born as the son of a man the world condemned. In his final moments he states that he has no regrets and thanks Luffy for being his brother.

Whitebeard, watching the death of his son, continues fighting despite sustaining catastrophic injuries. He is struck by hundreds of bullets and sword wounds and ultimately receives a magma strike from Akainu as well. Whitebeard dies standing upright — his body never falls forward, a testament to his reputation as a man who never turned his back on an enemy in his entire life. The Gorosei later confirm that despite the severity of his wounds, Whitebeard never once turned away from his opponents.

Aftermath

Shanks, one of the Four Emperors, arrives at Marineford with his crew to stop the battle. His arrival, along with the destruction already caused, prompts both sides to stand down. The Marines suffer enormous casualties. The Whitebeard Pirates and their allies retreat.

Luffy, devastated by the loss of Ace, collapses and loses consciousness. He is found by Trafalgar Law and his crew, who provide emergency medical treatment. Law's Devil Fruit, the Ope Ope no Mi (Op-Op Fruit), allows him to perform surgical operations that would be impossible by conventional medicine.

The war fundamentally reshapes the balance of power in the One Piece world. With Whitebeard dead, the territories he protected become unclaimed, triggering a wave of conflict known as the Scramble for Whitebeard's Territories. The event is considered the beginning of a new era in piracy.

Luffy later returns to Marineford alone to ring the Ox Bell sixteen times — eight times for the past and eight times for the future — a traditional gesture of mourning used by sailors. This is his way of saying goodbye to Ace and signaling his intention to become stronger before returning to the sea.
